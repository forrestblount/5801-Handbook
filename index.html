Things to do

— Oakland

Joaquin Miller Park

Known locally as "JMP," Joaquin Miller Park is a sprawling 500-acre urban wilderness in the Oakland hills that offers a perfect escape into nature without leaving the city. The park is named after the "Poet of the Sierras" and features miles of hiking trails winding through majestic redwood groves, eucalyptus trees, and rolling hills.

Montclair Farmer's Market

Held every Sunday morning, this vibrant market features local farmers selling fresh, seasonal produce, artisan breads, gourmet cheeses, organic eggs, and handmade crafts. If you'd prefer a larger market, check out the Grand Lake Farmer's market on Saturdays.

Temescal Alley

Enjoy a wide array of shops and restaurants in Temescal, one of my favorite neighborhoods to meander through.

Things to do

— Marin

Hiking Mt Tamalpais

One of my favorite places in the bay, hiking Mt Tam will take you through many environments and there's no better way to end the day than a sunset from East Peak.

Stinson Beach

Stinson Beach is the quintessential Northern California beach town—laid-back, beautiful, and perfect for a day of sun and surf. Rent a wet suit and boogie boards for an amazing surf experience.

Muir Woods

Muir Woods is home to some of the last remaining old-growth coastal redwood forests in the Bay Area. Walking among these ancient trees—some over 1,000 years old and 250 feet tall—is a humbling and awe-inspiring experience. The Cathedral Grove and Bootjack Trail offer accessible paths suitable for all fitness levels.

Things to do

— San Francisco

Ferry Building

I love being on the water in the bay and my favorite way of getting to SF's Ferry Building is by Ferry! Once you've arrived enjoy a vibrant marketplace featuring artisan food vendors, farm-fresh produce, and some of the Bay Area's best eateries.

SF MOMA

A fantastic museum in the heart of San Francisco, featuring works by Frida Kahlo, Jackson Pollock, Andy Warhol, and my favorite, Mark Rothko.

Land's End

On the far side of San Francisco, Land's End offers stunning views of the Golden Gate Bridge, the Marin Headlands, and the rugged Pacific coastline. This moderate trail winds through cypress forests, past historic ruins, and along cliffside paths that make you feel miles away from the city while still within its limits.

Fireplace

Usage

Before: Open the flue
During: Never leave the fire unattended
After: Close the flue, replace firewood, restore items

Firewood

Firewood is stored by the back door at the bottom of the stairs or in the pile along the driveway. Only use wood that is completely dry. If you need to resupply, firewood can be purchased at Lucky's in Montclair.

The Flue

The flue controls the airflow to the chimney. It must be open before lighting your fire and closed before you go to bed. The lever is inside the fireplace on the right and you will feel airflow when it is open. Push the lever up to open the flue. To close, pull the lever down.

The Fire Screen

Once your fire is lit, please close the metal screen. This screen prevents sparks from escaping onto the carpet or furniture.

Espresso

— Breville Barista Express
Need help? Download the BILT App for interactive tutorials and step-by-step guides.
Good Morning!

Daily Use

  1. Fill water tank (back of machine)
  2. Add beans to hopper on top
  3. Grind into portafilter → tamp → lock in
  4. Press 1 CUP or 2 CUP to brew
  5. Steam milk (optional): turn steam dial right, wait for light, froth, then turn off

Maintenance

The Clean Light is Flashing

  1. Fill water container
  2. Drop 1 cleaning tablet in the espresso basket with the rubber plug inserted
  3. Turn off machine (if on)
  4. Place pitcher under to catch water
  5. Hold 1 CUP + 2 CUP buttons for 2 seconds, then hold power until it turns on — the clean light will flash
  6. Wait about 5 minutes for the cycle to finish

The Clean Light is Solid

  1. Remove the filter
  2. Add 4 oz descaler solution to the water tank
  3. Hold 2 CUP button, then press power to turn on
  4. Manually cycle through all water dispensing options 2–3 times
  5. Run plain water through until no vinegar taste remains
  6. Replace the filter
